# German Banking Requirements for Business Plans

## IHK (Industrie- und Handelskammer) Guidelines

### Standard Business Plan Structure
The IHK recommends the following structure for business plans:

1. **Zusammenfassung (Executive Summary)**
   - Brief overview of business concept
   - Key success factors
   - Financing requirements

2. **Gründerperson / Management (Founder/Management)**
   - Qualifications and experience
   - Motivation and goals
   - Team structure

3. **Geschäftsidee (Business Idea)**
   - Product/service description
   - Customer benefit and USP
   - Innovation level

4. **Markt und Wettbewerb (Market and Competition)**
   - Market size and growth
   - Target customer analysis
   - Competitive landscape
   - Market entry barriers

5. **Marketing und Vertrieb (Marketing and Sales)**
   - Pricing strategy
   - Distribution channels
   - Marketing measures
   - Sales projections

6. **Unternehmensorganisation (Company Organization)**
   - Legal form
   - Location
   - Personnel planning
   - Suppliers and partners

7. **Finanzplanung (Financial Planning)**
   - Capital requirements (Kapitalbedarf)
   - Financing plan (Finanzierungsplan)
   - Profitability forecast (Rentabilitätsvorschau)
   - Liquidity planning (Liquiditätsplanung)
   - Balance sheet planning (Bilanzplanung)

8. **SWOT-Analyse**
   - Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats

9. **Chancen und Risiken (Opportunities and Risks)**
   - Risk assessment
   - Mitigation strategies

## KfW (Kreditanstalt für Wiederaufbau) Requirements

### Key Focus Areas for KfW Loan Applications

1. **Sustainability and Viability**
   - Proof of long-term business viability
   - Realistic market assumptions
   - Clear competitive advantages

2. **Financial Requirements**
   - Detailed capital requirements breakdown
   - Clear use of funds
   - Equity contribution (typically 10-20% minimum)
   - 3-year financial projections minimum

3. **Specific Financial Statements Required**
   - Investitionsplan (Investment Plan)
   - Kapitalbedarfsplan (Capital Requirements Plan)
   - Finanzierungsplan (Financing Plan)
   - Umsatzplanung (Sales Planning)
   - Kostenplanung (Cost Planning)
   - Liquiditätsplanung (Liquidity Planning)
   - Rentabilitätsvorschau (Profitability Forecast)

4. **Risk Assessment**
   - Sensitivity analysis
   - Break-even calculation
   - Debt service capacity (Schuldendienstfähigkeit)

## Sparkasse Evaluation Criteria

### What Sparkasse Banks Look For

1. **Creditworthiness Assessment**
   - Personal credit history (Schufa)
   - Business experience in the sector
   - Equity ratio
   - Collateral availability

2. **Business Plan Quality Indicators**
   - Realistic assumptions
   - Market knowledge demonstrated
   - Clear revenue model
   - Conservative financial projections
   - Professional presentation

3. **Key Financial Ratios**
   - Eigenkapitalquote (Equity ratio): >20% preferred
   - Debt service coverage ratio: >1.2
   - Break-even point: Within 18-24 months
   - Liquidity reserve: 3-6 months operating costs

4. **Documentation Standards**
   - Clear, professional formatting
   - Consistent numbering and structure
   - All assumptions documented
   - Sources cited for market data
   - Appendices with supporting documents

## Retail Sector Specific Considerations

### Market Analysis Requirements
- German retail market size and trends
- E-commerce vs. brick-and-mortar analysis
- Consumer behavior data
- Seasonal variations
- Regional market differences

### Regulatory Compliance
- Handelsgesetzbuch (HGB) compliance
- Consumer protection laws (Verbraucherschutzgesetz)
- Data protection (DSGVO/GDPR)
- Distance selling regulations (Fernabsatzgesetz)
- Product liability (Produkthaftungsgesetz)

### Typical Startup Costs for Retail
- Initial inventory: €20,000-€50,000
- Website/E-commerce platform: €5,000-€15,000
- Marketing launch: €10,000-€25,000
- Legal/administrative: €3,000-€5,000
- Working capital reserve: 3-6 months operating costs

### Key Performance Indicators for Retail
- Gross margin: 40-60% typical for fashion retail
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Customer lifetime value (CLV)
- Inventory turnover: 4-6x per year
- Return rate: 20-30% for online fashion
- Conversion rate: 1-3% for e-commerce

## Financial Model Best Practices

### Assumptions Sheet Must Include
- Revenue assumptions (units, pricing, growth rates)
- Cost of goods sold (COGS) percentages
- Operating expense categories and growth
- Personnel costs with FTE planning
- Marketing spend as % of revenue
- Working capital requirements
- Depreciation schedules
- Tax rates and assumptions
- Loan terms and interest rates

### Three-Statement Model Integration
- Income statement drives cash flow
- Cash flow impacts balance sheet
- Balance sheet must balance
- All statements interconnected with formulas

### Sensitivity Analysis Variables
- Sales volume variance (+/- 20%)
- Pricing variance (+/- 10%)
- COGS variance (+/- 15%)
- Marketing efficiency variance
- Payment terms impact on cash flow

## Common Rejection Reasons

### What to Avoid
- Overly optimistic revenue projections
- Insufficient market research
- Unclear competitive differentiation
- Inadequate equity contribution
- Missing risk analysis
- Poor cash flow planning
- Unrealistic break-even timeline
- Lack of industry knowledge
- Incomplete financial statements
- Unprofessional presentation

## Language and Terminology

### Key German Financial Terms
- Gewinn- und Verlustrechnung (GuV) = Income Statement
- Liquiditätsplanung = Cash Flow Statement
- Bilanz = Balance Sheet
- Kapitalbedarf = Capital Requirements
- Eigenkapital = Equity
- Fremdkapital = Debt
- Umsatzerlöse = Revenue
- Wareneinsatz = Cost of Goods Sold
- Betriebsausgaben = Operating Expenses
- EBITDA = Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization
- Deckungsbeitrag = Contribution Margin
- Break-Even-Point = Break-Even Point
- Schuldendienstfähigkeit = Debt Service Capacity
